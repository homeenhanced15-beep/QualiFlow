## 3. Pennsylvania Statutory Compliance

### 3.1 Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law (73 P.S. §§ 201-1 et seq.)

Leads QualiFlow complies with the Pennsylvania Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law (UTPCPL). We represent that:
- All advertising and marketing materials regarding our lead generation services are truthful and not misleading.
- Our chatbots clearly identify themselves as lead qualification tools and do not engage in deceptive conduct.
- Lead quality representations are based on reasonable and verifiable criteria.
- Clients who believe they have been harmed by a violation of the UTPCPL may pursue remedies including actual damages or $100, whichever is greater, plus reasonable attorneys' fees, as provided under 73 P.S. § 201-9.2.

### 3.2 Breach of Personal Information Notification Act (73 P.S. §§ 2301-2329)

In compliance with Pennsylvania's data breach notification law:
- We maintain a written data security policy.
- In the event of a breach of personal information affecting Pennsylvania residents, we will notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay.
- If more than 1,000 Pennsylvania residents are affected, we will also notify the Pennsylvania Attorney General and the three major credit reporting agencies.
- Notification will include: description of the breach, types of information compromised, steps taken to prevent future breaches, and contact information for inquiries.

### 3.3 Telemarketer Registration Act (73 P.S. §§ 2241-2252)

As a lead generation service operating in Pennsylvania:
- We comply with Pennsylvania's Do Not Call registry requirements.
- We maintain and honor internal do-not-call lists.
- Automated dialing and prerecorded messages are used only in compliance with federal TCPA and Pennsylvania state law.
- Telemarketing calls are conducted only during permissible hours (8:00 AM - 9:00 PM local time).

### 3.4 Pennsylvania E-SIGN and Electronic Transaction Act (73 P.S. §§ 2260.301-2260.310)

Electronic signatures and records used in our platform:
- Have the same legal effect as written signatures and records.
- Are admissible as evidence in Pennsylvania courts.
- Comply with the requirements for electronic records retention.
- Clients consent to electronic delivery of documents, invoices, and notices.

### 3.5 Tax Reform Code — Sales and Use Tax (72 P.S. §§ 7201 et seq.)

- Digital services provided by Leads QualiFlow may be subject to Pennsylvania sales tax as determined by the Pennsylvania Department of Revenue.
- Applicable sales tax will be separately stated on invoices and remitted to the Pennsylvania Department of Revenue.
- Clients are responsible for providing valid exemption certificates if claiming tax-exempt status.
- Out-of-state clients may be responsible for use tax in their home state.

### 4.1 Formation and Enforceability
Contracts formed through our platform are legally binding under Pennsylvania contract law, which recognizes offer, acceptance, and consideration as the essential elements of an enforceable agreement.

### 4.2 Pennsylvania Uniform Commercial Code (13 Pa.C.S. §§ 1101 et seq.)
Our lead services are classified as "services" rather than "goods" under Pennsylvania law and are therefore governed by common law contract principles rather than Article 2 of the UCC.

### 4.3 Pennsylvania Uniform Arbitration Act (42 Pa.C.S. §§ 7301-7320)
The arbitration provisions in our Terms of Service are specifically enforceable under the Pennsylvania Uniform Arbitration Act. Arbitration shall be conducted in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, before a neutral arbitrator.

### 4.4 Pennsylvania Comparative Negligence (42 Pa.C.S. § 7102)
In any dispute, liability shall be apportioned according to Pennsylvania's comparative negligence principles. A plaintiff's recovery shall be reduced in proportion to their own negligence.

### 4.5 Pennsylvania Statute of Frauds (33 P.S. § 1)
Our contracts may be enforced regardless of the Statute of Frauds because electronic records exist documenting the agreement.
