## 1. Our Commitment

Leads QualiFlow ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all users, including individuals with disabilities. We continuously work to improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards to ensure our website, chatbot platform, and all digital services are accessible to the widest possible audience.

This statement reflects our commitment to compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act, and the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards.

## 2. Accessibility Standards We Follow

Our platform is designed, developed, and tested to conform with:

- **WCAG 2.1 Level AA:** The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines published by W3C serve as our primary accessibility benchmark.
- **ADA Title III:** We meet the requirements of Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act, which prohibits disability discrimination in public accommodations.
- **Section 508:** We implement standards equivalent to Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act for electronic and information technology.

## 3. Accessibility Features

### 3.1 Screen Reader Compatibility
- Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy (h1-h6).
- ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) labels and landmarks.
- Alt text for all meaningful images and icons.
- Descriptive link text (no "click here" without context).
- Proper form labels and error announcements.

### 3.2 Voice Navigation
- Full voice navigation capability using Web Speech API.
- Voice-activated chatbot interaction for lead qualification.
- Voice commands for navigation, form completion, and account management.
- Speech-to-text input for all text fields.

### 3.3 Keyboard Navigation
- Full keyboard navigability with logical tab order.
- Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements.
- No keyboard traps — navigate using Tab, Enter, Escape, and arrow keys.
- Skip-to-content links at the top of every page.

### 3.4 Captioning and Transcripts
- Captions for all video content.
- Transcripts for audio content and podcasts.
- Real-time captioning for live events and webinars.
- Text alternatives for chatbot voice interactions.

### 3.5 Visual Design
- Color contrast ratios meeting WCAG 2.1 AA requirements (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).
- No information conveyed solely through color.
- Resizable text up to 200% without loss of content.
- Consistent navigation and layout across all pages.

### 3.6 Multimedia
- Pause, stop, and hide controls for moving, blinking, or auto-updating content.
- No content flashing more than three times per second (seizure prevention).
- Audio descriptions for video content where needed.

## 4. Chatbot-Specific Accessibility

Our lead qualification chatbots include:
- Screen reader announcements for new messages and status changes.
- Keyboard-only operation for all chatbot interactions.
- Voice input and output for auditory interaction preference.
- Clear, simple language appropriate for general audiences.
- Ability to repeat or clarify questions.
- Option to transfer to a human representative at any point.
